            <div class="article__content">
              <p>Radiant skin isn't just about luck or genetics—it's about understanding how your skin works and giving it exactly what it needs to thrive. Let's dive into the science behind that coveted glow.</p>

<h2>Understanding Your Skin Barrier</h2>

<p>Your skin's outermost layer, the stratum corneum, acts as a protective barrier against environmental stressors while locking in moisture. When this barrier is healthy, your skin looks smooth, plump, and radiant. When it's compromised, you'll notice dullness, sensitivity, and dehydration. The key? Ingredients like ceramides, fatty acids, and cholesterol that reinforce this natural defense.</p>

<h2>The Power of Antioxidants</h2>

<p>Free radicals from UV exposure, pollution, and stress cause oxidative damage that leads to premature aging and dull skin. Antioxidants like vitamin C, vitamin E, and niacinamide neutralize these harmful molecules, protecting your skin and promoting a brighter, more even complexion. Think of them as your skin's personal bodyguards.</p>

<h2>Cell Turnover and Renewal</h2>

<p>Your skin naturally sheds dead cells and generates new ones, but this process slows with age. Ingredients like retinol, AHAs, and BHAs encourage faster cell turnover, revealing fresh, glowing skin underneath. Regular exfoliation—whether chemical or physical—helps maintain this renewal process for consistently radiant results.</p>

<h2>Hydration at the Cellular Level</h2>

<p>Hyaluronic acid can hold up to 1,000 times its weight in water, making it a hydration powerhouse. When your skin cells are properly hydrated, they appear fuller and more luminous. Pair humectants like hyaluronic acid with occlusives like squalane to draw in moisture and seal it in for lasting hydration.</p>

<h2>Collagen and Elasticity</h2>

<p>Collagen provides structure and firmness, while elastin gives skin its bounce. As we age, production of both decreases, leading to sagging and fine lines. Peptides, retinoids, and vitamin C stimulate collagen synthesis, helping maintain that youthful, radiant appearance.</p>

<h2>The Bottom Line</h2>

<p>Radiant skin is the result of a healthy skin barrier, protected cells, efficient renewal, deep hydration, and strong structural support. When you understand the science, you can make informed choices about what your skin truly needs.</p>
