## Problem Statement

- `Verified`: The current micro-slice runtime protocol has successfully proven utility, trust, story, process, editorial-quote, and one content-card structure.
- `Verified`: The next attempted content-and-blog candidate, minimal blog highlights, was rejected as insufficiently distinct from `stone-content-card-strip.liquid`.

## Why the current protocol is tightening

1. `Verified`: The current protocol intentionally excludes live objects, JS-driven interaction, protected surfaces, block files, second sections, second templates, and broader composition.
2. `Inference`: Those exclusions were useful for proving the base platform safely, but they now make many remaining family candidates collapse into either duplication or dishonest placeholders.
3. `Inference`: Continuing to force runtime slices under the same constraints risks section sprawl without meaningful capability gain.

## Decision

- `Verified`: Do not authorize another bounded runtime implementation unit immediately after `UNIT-064`.
- `Verified`: Treat the current micro-slice runway as **locally exhausted** under the present constraints.
- `Verified`: Shift the next governed unit into a docs-only controlled-expansion proposal rather than another runtime proof attempt.
