# Phase 2 Minimal Utility Spacer Risks v1

## Primary Risks

1. `Inference`: Semantic drift, where a neutral spacer turns into a promo lane, trust separator, support gap helper, or announcement scaffold.
2. `Inference`: Redundancy drift, where the slice becomes an unbounded restatement of the divider-band proof instead of a bounded adjustable-spacing proof.
3. `Inference`: Protected-surface drift, where the section starts demanding shared-shell, runtime-JS, global-settings, or layout-level edits.
4. `Inference`: Scope creep, where implementation introduces blocks, helper snippets, or extra templates to make spacing behavior more elaborate.
5. `Inference`: Locale drift, where Hebrew and English schema additions stop matching structurally because of rushed naming or extra options.

## Mitigations

- `Verified`: Keep the implementation inside the approved five runtime files only.
- `Verified`: Keep the spacer neutral and structural, with bounded size controls rather than content or conversion semantics.
- `Verified`: Reject promo framing, reassurance framing, support states, announcement behavior, and multi-section orchestration inside this slice.
- `Verified`: Reuse `stone-section-shell` unchanged and stop immediately if that becomes insufficient.
- `Verified`: Check locale subtree parity before accepting the unit.

## Failure Triggers

- `Verified`: Any protected-surface edit.
- `Verified`: Any second section or second template.
- `Verified`: Any `project/blocks/` addition.
- `Verified`: Any JS-driven behavior.
- `Verified`: Any trust, support, promo, campaign, or announcement vocabulary in the section or template.
